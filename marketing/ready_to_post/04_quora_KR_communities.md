### Q. "What is the difference between Western astrology and Korean/Chinese astrology?"
Western astrology is built around your Sun sign — the zodiac constellation the Sun was in during your birth month. It's one data point.

Korean Saju (사주) / Chinese BaZi uses four "pillars": the year, month, day, and hour of birth, each expressed as a stem-branch pair. That's eight characters total (BaZi = "eight characters"). The most important is your Day Master — the heavenly stem of your birth day, which represents "you."

So two people born in the same month share a Sun sign but can have completely different Saju charts. If you want to see your own four-pillar chart in English (free, no signup), this calculates it: https://tarofortune.pythonanywhere.com/en/

### Q. "What does it mean to get the Death card in tarot?"
Almost never literal death. The Death card means transformation — the definitive end of a phase so something new can begin. Reversed, it often signals resistance to a change that needs to happen, which prolongs the discomfort. Full meaning here: https://tarofortune.pythonanywhere.com/en/tarot/death
